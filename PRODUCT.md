# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Ambitious professionals aged 22–40 — founders, operators, early hires, career-switchers — who want a confident, shareable way to understand and articulate how they work. They take the quiz on their phone or laptop, often share the result on LinkedIn or in team Slack channels. The result card is the product; it has to feel worth sharing.

## Product Purpose

Profyle is a 5-minute professional personality quiz that outputs a two-word type (e.g. "The Quiet Builder") from a system of 5 archetypes and 4 prefixes — 20 possible results, each with a unique fingerprint. The quiz reveals how someone works, who they work best with, and where they belong. The result page and shareable card are the primary deliverable. Success means high share rate and high completion rate.

## Brand Personality

Confident · Restrained · Precise

Profyle feels like something a McKinsey analyst and a creative director built together. Smart, editorial, not loud. The copy is sharp and flattering. The quiz doesn't feel like a quiz — it feels like a conversation. The result doesn't feel like a label — it feels like a mirror.

## Anti-references

- BuzzFeed / PlayBuzz — no playful, disposable, low-stakes quiz energy
- Corporate HR / DISC / StrengthsFinder UI — no clinical, cold, enterprise-assessment feel
- Generic SaaS startup — no gradient heroes, floating illustrations, same-same B2B landing page
- Warm-cream / sand / beige AI defaults — pastel accents carry the warmth, not the background

## Design Principles

1. **The result is the product.** Every design decision serves the moment someone sees their type for the first time and the moment they share it.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** White space is doing work. Don't fill it. A quiet design reads as more premium than a loud one.
3. **Pastels earn their place.** Pastel colors are used as archetype-specific accents and UI highlights — not as decoration. Each archetype has a hue. It's a system, not a mood.
4. **The quiz feels like a conversation.** One question at a time, clean options, no clutter. The experience should feel effortless and fast.
5. **Words do the heavy lifting.** The copy is already exceptional. Typography and spacing must honor it — generous leading, strong hierarchy, nothing fighting the words.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (aim for AAA on body text)
- Reduced motion respected — no mandatory animations blocking content
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable
- Sufficient contrast on all pastel-tinted surfaces (verify each archetype color)
